Introduction: A little about me.

I am originally from Cape Girardeau MO. In college I picked up playing disc golf as a way to get outside with some friends. After college I moved to St. Louis and then to Blue Springs. These are some of the course I liked best from around the state.

Fun courses in Southeast Missouri.

We'll start our trip in the place most of you will likely never go. It's 5 hours away, and you would probably only pass through here if you are trying to get to Memphis. Feel free to skip this section.

Cape County Park

This was the first course I ever played. It has a lot of holes in the woods; which is not kind to beginners. My disc would bounce off trees like a pinball. Still, this is one of my favorite courses. Most of the holes are fun, and the park is very pretty. Lots of rude geese though.

Scott County Park

A 10 minute drive south of Cape is Scott City. This course has a good mix of holes in the woods and long drives. A word of warning though; Some of the later holes have pretty steep drop offs. They can be frustrating, and you might lose a disc.

Fun Courses in St. Louis

Next up is St. Louis. Not much to say about the place. They have a good baseball team (usually) and a hockey team that finally managed to win the Stanley cup. Don't think I'm knocking the blues though. They once had Brett Hull AND Wayne Gretzky. Pretty rad.

Carrolton Disc Golf Course

This one is neat. The city of St. Louis was trying to expand the airport, so they drove thousands of people out of the Carrolton subdivision. They then never developed it and decided to put in a disc golf course. The whole park is full of flat terraces that go up and down hills. You can tell where houses used to stand. It feels like playing in a weird ghost town. Also, planes fly over like every 20 minutes.

Jefferson Barracks Park

This park is a bit hilly. Pack some water, and maybe some almonds. The park is set up to use the hills for interesting holes. I really like this one. Bonus: If you go near sunset, a scary amount of deer come out and just start grazing. They aren't afraid of humans, and that scares me a little bit. Fun Fact: More people die from deer than sharks in a given year. It's mostly car accidents, but still.

Fun courses in the Kansas City area

Finally some information you can use. If you read through all of the other entries, congratulations.

Water Works

When you ask around what peoples' favorite course is in KC, this is one that comes up a lot. It has a nice view looking over the city. It uses wooded areas really well. It is an absolute blast. The only bad thing is how many steep hills there are. Like climbing straight up a hill, steep. The first time I played it I wasn't expecting it, and I could only get through the front 9. I would still go back though. It is one of the prettiest courses I've been to.

Legacy Park

This one is my favorite in the area. The front 9 has a lot of long bombs. The back 9 are mostly technical shots in a nice shady area. It has a little of something for everyone. It isn't too hilly. It has a lot of good shade. It is hard to lose a disc. If you are looking for a place to start, I'd start here.

Final Thoughts

There are so many more great courses out there. If you've never given disc golf a shot, I hope this has made you reconsider. A set of discs will set you back 30 bucks, but then you get to explore some parks in a truly unique way.
